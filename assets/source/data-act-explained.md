# Data Act Explained — Commission overview page (snapshot)

The Commission maintains a public-facing overview page at:

`https://digital-strategy.ec.europa.eu/en/policies/data-act`

This page is non-authoritative and its content is updated periodically. It is useful for:

- High-level orientation when first reading into the regulation.
- Confirmation of current applicability dates.
- Pointers to related policy documents (Data Governance Act, Open Data Directive, etc.).

The page is **not** a primary source. For legal drafting and analysis the Skill quotes the regulation and the FAQ directly, not the overview page.

---

## Snapshot of key content (as at v1 release of this Skill)

### Key dates the page highlights

The overview page consistently restates the four Article 50 dates plus the entry-into-force date. When reasoning about timeline questions, prefer Article 50 itself (in `regulation-2023-2854.md`) over this overview.

### Scope summary the page provides

The page emphasises that the Data Act:

- Applies to connected products and related services placed on the EU market.
- Provides access rights to users of those products and services.
- Imposes switching, portability, and interoperability obligations on cloud / SaaS providers.
- Coexists with GDPR (which prevails on personal data).
- Does not displace sectoral law.

### Related Commission documents the page links

- The text of Regulation (EU) 2023/2854 on EUR-Lex.
- The FAQ on the Data Act (current version).
- The Recommendation on standard contractual clauses for data sharing and cloud computing contracts.
- The Data Governance Act (Regulation (EU) 2022/868).
- The Open Data Directive (Directive (EU) 2019/1024).
- Sectoral guidance documents as published.

### Useful for non-legal stakeholders

The page is written for a general audience and is appropriate to refer business stakeholders to. It is **not** appropriate to cite as authority in a legal deliverable.
