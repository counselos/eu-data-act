**Drafting notes (for the lawyer; remove before publication)**

- This page satisfies the Article 29(6) public-availability duty and supports the Article 29(4) pre-contract information duty. It does not, on its own, replace the pre-contract disclosure required by Article 29(4) — that disclosure must be made before contract conclusion, in a form the customer can retain.
- The methodology in section 4 is the direct-cost demonstration. A bare statement of the charge without methodology will not satisfy regulator scrutiny if challenged. Keep the methodology current.
- Section 5 must be revised before 12 January 2027 to remove references to switching charges. Section 4 should also be removed at that time.
- Section 6 is conditional. Where applicable, be specific about the technical reason. A vague "switching is complex" statement creates litigation risk and may be treated as a non-compliant disclosure.
- Where the Provider's services include those out of scope of Chapter VI under Article 31 (custom-built or non-production), Article 29 does not apply to those services. The page should clearly identify any service in that situation and disapply this page accordingly.
- Sectoral overlays (DORA for financial-services customers, NIS2 for essential entities) may impose additional disclosures.
