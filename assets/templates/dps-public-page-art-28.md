## 3. Procedure on receipt of a third-country governmental order

If we receive a third-country court, tribunal or administrative authority order requiring transfer of or access to non-personal data held in the Union, we proceed as follows:

1. We assess whether the order is based on an international agreement (e.g., a mutual legal assistance treaty) in force between the requesting third country and the Union or with the relevant Member State (Article 32(2)).
2. Where no such agreement applies and compliance would risk conflict with Union or Member-State law, we assess whether all of the cumulative conditions of Article 32(3) are met:
   - the third-country system requires the reasons and proportionality of the decision to be set out and requires the decision to be specific in character;
   - the addressee may bring a reasoned objection before a competent third-country court;
   - that court can take into account the relevant legal interests protected by Union or Member-State law.
3. Where transfer or access takes place, we provide only the minimum amount of data permissible (Article 32(4)).
4. We inform the customer of the request before complying, except where the request serves law-enforcement purposes and notification would defeat that purpose (Article 32(5)).
