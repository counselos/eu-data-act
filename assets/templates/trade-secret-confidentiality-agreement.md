# Trade-Secret Confidentiality Measures — Agreement

**Article 4(6) (and 5(9)) of Regulation (EU) 2023/2854**

> **STARTING-POINT NOTICE.** This is a starting-point draft of the confidentiality measures the Data Holder and the User (or third party) agree before disclosure of trade-secret-protected data. Substantive review by qualified counsel is required before any reliance, signing, dispatch, or filing.

---

**This agreement** between **[INSERT — Data Holder]** ("**Data Holder**") and **[INSERT — User or Third Party]** ("**Recipient**"), dated **[INSERT — DD MMM YYYY]**, sets out the proportionate technical and organisational measures the parties agree to preserve the confidentiality of trade-secret-protected data made available pursuant to Article 4 (or, as the case may be, Article 5) of Regulation (EU) 2023/2854 (the "**Data Act**").

---

## 1. Purpose

The Data Holder is required by Article 4(1) (or 5(1)) of the Data Act to make readily available data accessible to the Recipient. Some of that data is protected as trade secrets within the meaning of Article 2(18) of the Data Act and Directive (EU) 2016/943. The parties enter this agreement under Article 4(6) (or, where the Recipient is a third party, Article 5(9)) to agree the measures necessary to preserve confidentiality before disclosure.

## 2. Identification of trade-secret data

The data identified as trade-secret-protected, including the metadata fields used to mark it, is set out in **Schedule A**. The Data Holder confirms that all data designated in Schedule A meets the definition of "trade secret" in Article 2(18) of the Data Act.

The Data Holder will identify trade-secret-protected data in the relevant metadata of any data made available to the Recipient.

## 3. Recipient's confidentiality undertaking

The Recipient undertakes to preserve the confidentiality of the trade-secret-protected data and to apply the measures set out in this agreement.

The Recipient shall not:

- (a) disclose trade-secret-protected data to any third party except as permitted by this agreement;
- (b) use trade-secret-protected data for any purpose other than the purpose specified in **Schedule B**; or
- (c) reverse-engineer, decompile, or otherwise seek to derive the underlying methods, algorithms, or processes from trade-secret-protected data.

[For Article 4(10) where Recipient is the User:]

- (d) use trade-secret-protected data to develop a connected product that competes with the connected product from which the data originate, nor share with a third party with that intent (Article 4(10) of the Data Act).

## 4. Technical measures

The Recipient shall implement and maintain at least the following technical measures:

- (a) Encryption of trade-secret-protected data in transit and at rest using current industry-standard algorithms.
- (b) Access control: trade-secret-protected data shall be accessible only to identified individuals on a need-to-know basis. Access shall be authenticated and logged.
- (c) Segregation: trade-secret-protected data shall be stored separately from data that is not trade-secret-protected, and tagged in storage to support that segregation.
- (d) Audit logs of access events shall be retained for at least [INSERT — period, e.g., 24 months] and made available to the Data Holder on request.
- (e) Technical Protection Measures (Article 11 of the Data Act) applied by the Data Holder shall be respected by the Recipient.

## 5. Organisational measures

The Recipient shall:

- (a) maintain a written information-security policy that addresses trade-secret-protected data;
- (b) train personnel with access to trade-secret-protected data on its protection;
- (c) bind personnel to confidentiality obligations of equivalent stringency to this agreement; and
- (d) where applicable, bind sub-contractors to obligations of equivalent stringency before any disclosure to them.

## 6. Onward disclosure

The Recipient may disclose trade-secret-protected data to:

- (a) authorised employees on a need-to-know basis under section 5(c);
- (b) sub-contractors bound under section 5(d), with the Data Holder's prior written approval (not unreasonably withheld); and
- (c) regulators or courts where compelled by law, with prompt notification to the Data Holder unless prohibited by law.

Onward disclosure to third parties not covered by (a) to (c) is prohibited.

## 7. Breach notification

The Recipient shall notify the Data Holder of any breach or suspected breach of confidentiality of trade-secret-protected data without undue delay and in any event within [INSERT — period, e.g., 72 hours] of becoming aware. The notification shall include:

- (a) a description of the breach;
- (b) the categories and approximate volume of data affected;
- (c) the measures taken or proposed to remediate; and
- (d) a contact point for further information.

## 8. Remedies

If the Recipient fails to implement the agreed measures or undermines the confidentiality of trade-secret-protected data:

- (a) the Data Holder may withhold or suspend further data sharing in writing without undue delay (Article 4(7) / 5(10) of the Data Act), and shall notify the competent authority designated under Article 37;
- (b) where the Data Holder is also a trade-secret holder and is highly likely to suffer serious and irreparable economic loss notwithstanding the measures, the Data Holder may, on a case-by-case basis, refuse access to specific data in question and shall substantiate that refusal in writing without undue delay (Article 4(8) / 5(11));
- (c) the Data Holder retains all remedies available under Directive (EU) 2016/943 and applicable national law for misappropriation of trade secrets.

## 9. Duration

This agreement enters into force on the date stated above and remains in force for as long as the trade-secret-protected data identified in Schedule A continues to qualify as a trade secret under Article 2(18) of the Data Act.

**Drafting notes (for the lawyer; remove before signature)**

- The Schedule A justifications must address the three-element test in Article 2(18) (Directive (EU) 2016/943 Article 2(1)): (i) secret, (ii) commercial value because secret, (iii) reasonable steps taken by the holder to keep it secret. A trade-secret claim that fails any one element does not qualify.
- The breach-notification window in section 7 should align with the broader information-security policy of the parties.
- The remedies in section 8 must reflect the Article 4(7) / 4(8) procedure correctly — withhold/suspend first; refusal only on case-by-case demonstration of serious + irreparable economic loss.
- Where the Recipient is a third party under Article 5, Article 5(9)–(11) mirror the operative provisions; cite those instead.
- Where the Recipient is also subject to GDPR as a controller / processor, this agreement does not displace the data processing agreement.
