**Drafting notes (for the lawyer; remove before issuance)**

- Verify that all nine Article 3(3) items (a)–(i) are addressed, in distinct sections, with no item omitted. A pre-contract notice missing any item is non-compliant.
- Section 4 (Article 3(3)(c)) is often under-specified. State purposes specifically — "service operation" alone is too generic. The user must understand what the Provider will do with the data and which third parties will use it.
- Section 7 should mirror the actual portal / process. Boilerplate that does not match real practice will fail in dispute.
- Section 9 trade-secret declaration should be accurate at the time of issuance and revised if the trade-secret position changes.
- Where the Related Service is also a DPS, the Article 25 contract and Article 26 / 28 / 29 obligations apply additionally; this notice does not displace them.
- Consider whether GDPR Article 13 / 14 transparency obligations are also engaged for personal data.
- For services placed on the market after 12 September 2026, the Article 3(1) by-design obligation also applies.
