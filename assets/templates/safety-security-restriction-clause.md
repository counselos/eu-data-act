## Clause [X]. Restriction of access on safety or security grounds — Article 4(2)

### [X].1 Conditions

The Data Holder and the User may, by mutual agreement, restrict or prohibit accessing, using or further sharing of specific data where all of the following cumulative conditions are met:

- (a) processing of that data could undermine a security requirement of the Connected Product laid down by Union or national law;
- (b) the undermining would result in a serious adverse effect on the health, safety or security of natural persons;
- (c) the restriction is set out in writing in this contract; and
- (d) where the Data Holder refuses to share data under this clause, it notifies the competent authority designated under Article 37 of the Data Act.

### [X].2 Identified restrictions

The data categories subject to this clause and the underlying Union or national security requirements are listed in **Schedule [Y]**. Each entry sets out:

- the data category;
- the Union or national security requirement engaged;
- the causal link between disclosure and undermining the requirement; and
- the natural persons whose health, safety or security would be affected.

### [X].3 Procedure on refusal

If, on the facts of a specific request, the Data Holder concludes that the conditions in [X].1 are met, the Data Holder shall:

- (a) refuse the request in writing to the User without undue delay, citing this clause and identifying the data category and the security requirement engaged;
- (b) notify the competent authority designated under Article 37 of the Data Act; and
- (c) preserve a record of the substantiation for inspection.

### [X].4 What this clause is not

This clause is not:

- (a) a generic security excuse or category-wide exclusion;
- (b) a substitute for the Article 4(6)–(8) (or 5(9)–(11)) trade-secret regime;
- (c) applicable to data subjects' GDPR Articles 15 and 20 rights, which are not displaced by this clause; or
- (d) applicable retroactively to requests made before this clause is in force.

**Drafting notes (for the lawyer; remove before signature)**

- The cumulative conditions in [X].1 are strict. The Article 4(2) carve-out is narrow. A clause that purports to grant blanket discretion to the Data Holder is unlikely to be enforceable.
- Schedule [Y] must be populated with **specific** data categories and **identifiable** legal requirements. "Security" alone is not enough.
- Where sectoral law (NIS2, MDR, type-approval, DORA) provides the underlying security requirement, cite the specific provision. The skill flags that sectoral lex specialis is not covered by it; the lawyer must verify independently.
- Section [X].3 procedural items must match internal SOPs. The competent-authority notification must be made — not merely promised.
- Cross-check with the trade-secret regime (Article 4(6)–(8)). The grounds are distinct; both can apply in the alternative on the same facts.
