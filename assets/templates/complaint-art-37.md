**Drafting notes (for the lawyer; remove before dispatch)**

- Verify the correct competent authority for the relevant Member State by reference to the Commission's published Article 37 list. Member States may designate multiple authorities; ensure the right one is targeted.
- Where multiple Member States are implicated, the complainant has a choice of forum (habitual residence, place of work, place of establishment). Choose with care — the Member State chosen affects which national procedural and remedial regime applies.
- Where the complaint involves personal data, consider whether a parallel GDPR complaint (to the supervisory authority) is appropriate. The two regimes operate in parallel.
- Section 6 must articulate the specific operative obligation and the specific conduct. Conclusory complaints are likely to be dismissed.
- Annex exhibits must be authentic and legible. Where confidentiality is required, mark accordingly and consider whether a reduced public version is needed.
- Sectoral overlays may engage different authorities (NIS2 cybersecurity authority, DORA financial-sector authorities). The lawyer must verify independently.
