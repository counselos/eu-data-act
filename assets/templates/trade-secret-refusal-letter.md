**Drafting notes (for the lawyer; remove before dispatch)**

- The procedural prerequisites of Article 4(6) and 4(7) must have been satisfied. Skipping straight to Article 4(8) is procedurally defective; an Article 38 complaint will probably succeed in that case.
- Section 3 substantiation must be on objective elements. Conclusory assertions ("disclosure would cause serious damage") are insufficient. Where possible, attach exhibits supporting the substantiation.
- Section 3.5 should articulate why the loss is **serious AND irreparable**. "Serious" alone is not enough (Recital 31).
- Section 4 must scope the refusal narrowly. A blanket category-wide refusal is unlikely to satisfy "case-by-case basis."
- Section 5 must be true. The competent authority must actually be notified, with a reference number where available, before this letter is dispatched.
- Confirm internally that the disclosure to the user / third party in section 3 does not itself disclose the trade secret.
- For a third-party request, cite Article 5(11) and the corresponding paragraph 5(9) where references say "Article 4(6)".
- For a user who is a data subject, recall that Recital 31 final sentence preserves GDPR data-subject rights regardless of this refusal.
