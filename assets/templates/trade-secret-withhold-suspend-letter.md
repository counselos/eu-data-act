**Drafting notes (for the lawyer; remove before dispatch)**

- The choice between "withhold" and "suspend" matters: "withhold" implies non-disclosure of the specified data; "suspend" implies a temporary halt to ongoing or streamed sharing. Be explicit about which.
- The action must be proportionate. If only one data element is affected, withhold only that element. Blanket withholding across all categories will be vulnerable to complaint.
- Annex 1 should be specific. Conclusory statements weaken the position on any subsequent dispute.
- The competent-authority notification must be made — not promised. Verify it has been sent before dispatch of this letter.
- This letter does not pre-empt or replace an Article 4(8) refusal. If after this Step 2 the trigger remains unresolved AND the conditions of Article 4(8) (highly likely serious and irreparable economic loss) are met, a separate Step 3 refusal letter may be appropriate.
- For a third-party recipient, cite Article 5(10) instead of Article 4(7) and adjust the references throughout.
