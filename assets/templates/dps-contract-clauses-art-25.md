# DPS Contract — Switching, Portability and Termination Clauses

**Article 25 of Regulation (EU) 2023/2854**

> **STARTING-POINT NOTICE.** This is a starting-point draft of the load-bearing switching clauses for a data-processing-service contract. The clauses are intended to satisfy Article 25(1)–(5). Substantive review by qualified counsel is required before any reliance, signing, dispatch, or filing. Drafters should also align with the Commission Recommendation on standard contractual clauses for cloud computing contracts (19 November 2025).

## Clause [X]. Switching and exit rights

### [X].1 Right to switch

The Customer may, at any time, switch from the Service to a data processing service of the same service type provided by a different provider, or to an on-premises ICT infrastructure, or use multiple providers of data processing services in parallel.

### [X].2 Notice period

The Customer shall initiate the switching process by notice to the Provider. The notice period shall not exceed **two (2) months** from the Customer's notice. (Article 25(2)(d).)

### [X].3 Mandatory transitional period

Upon expiry of the notice period referred to in [X].2, a mandatory transitional period of **30 calendar days** commences. During the transitional period, this contract remains in force and the Provider shall:

- (a) provide reasonable assistance to the Customer and any third party authorised by the Customer in the switching process;
- (b) act with due care to maintain business continuity, and continue the provision of the functions or services under this contract;
- (c) provide clear information concerning known risks to continuity in the provision of the functions or services on the part of the Provider; and
- (d) ensure that a high level of security is maintained throughout the switching process, in particular the security of data during transfer and the continued security of data during the retrieval period set out in [X].6.

(Article 25(2)(a)(i)–(iv).)

### [X].4 Customer's right to extend the transitional period

Without prejudice to [X].5, the Customer may extend the transitional period referred to in [X].3 once, for a period the Customer considers more appropriate for the Customer's purposes. (Article 25(5).)

### [X].5 Provider's right to extend the transitional period on grounds of technical infeasibility

Where the 30-day transitional period in [X].3 is technically infeasible, the Provider shall notify the Customer **within fourteen (14) working days** of the Customer's switching request, duly justifying the technical infeasibility in writing and indicating an alternative transitional period **not exceeding seven (7) months**. The Provider shall ensure service continuity throughout the alternative transitional period. (Article 25(4).)

### [X].6 Minimum data-retrieval period

The Provider shall provide a data-retrieval period of **at least 30 calendar days**, starting after the termination of the transitional period under [X].3 (or, where applicable, the alternative transitional period under [X].5). During the retrieval period, the Customer may retrieve all categories of data and digital assets specified in [X].8. (Article 25(2)(g).)

### [X].7 Erasure on termination

Upon expiry of the retrieval period in [X].6 (or, where the parties so agree in writing, an alternative later date), the Provider shall fully erase all exportable data and digital assets generated directly by the Customer or relating directly to the Customer, provided the switching process has completed successfully. (Article 25(2)(h).)

### [X].8 Categories of data and digital assets that may be ported

The exhaustive categories of data and digital assets that the Customer may port during the switching process are listed in **Schedule [Y]** to this contract. Schedule [Y] includes, at a minimum, all exportable data within the meaning of Article 2(38). (Article 25(2)(e).)

### [X].9 Carve-out for provider-internal data

The exhaustive categories of provider-internal data that are exempted from porting where there is a risk of trade-secret breach are listed in **Schedule [Z]**. The exemptions in Schedule [Z] **shall not** impede or delay the switching process. (Article 25(2)(f).)

### [X].10 Customer's options at end of notice

Upon termination of the notice period referred to in [X].2, the Customer may notify the Provider of its decision to:

- (a) switch to a different provider of data processing services, in which case the Customer shall provide the necessary details of that provider;
- (b) switch to an on-premises ICT infrastructure; or
- (c) erase its exportable data and digital assets.

(Article 25(3).)

### [X].11 Termination

This contract is considered terminated and the Customer will be notified of termination:

- (a) upon successful completion of the switching process; or
- (b) at the end of the notice period referred to in [X].2 where the Customer chooses option [X].10(c) (erasure rather than switch).

(Article 25(2)(c).)

### [X].12 Exit-strategy support

The Provider undertakes to support the Customer's exit strategy relevant to the contracted services, including by providing all relevant information requested by the Customer for that purpose. (Article 25(2)(b).)

### [X].13 Switching charges

The Provider may impose switching charges only as permitted by Article 29 of the Data Act:

- From **12 January 2027**, no switching charges may be imposed for the switching process. (Article 29(1).)
- Until **12 January 2027**, reduced switching charges may be imposed; such charges shall not exceed the costs the Provider incurs that are directly linked to the switching process. (Article 29(2)–(3).)

The Provider's standard service fees and any early-termination penalties under this contract are set out in **Schedule [Q]** and are not switching charges within the meaning of Article 29.

### [X].14 Good-faith cooperation

The Provider, the Customer, and any destination provider of data processing services shall cooperate in good faith to make the switching process effective, enable the timely transfer of data, and maintain the continuity of the data processing service. (Article 27.)

### [X].15 Open interfaces and interoperability

[For PaaS / SaaS only — adapt or omit if the service falls under Article 30(1) IaaS regime.]

The Provider shall make open interfaces available to the Customer and any destination provider, free of charge, to facilitate the switching process. The interfaces shall include sufficient information to enable the development of software to communicate with the Service for the purposes of data portability and interoperability. The Provider shall ensure compatibility with common specifications based on open interoperability specifications or harmonised standards published in the central Union standards repository, at least 12 months after such references are published. (Articles 30(2)–(3).)

Where common specifications or harmonised standards have not been published, the Provider shall, on the Customer's request, export all exportable data in a structured, commonly used and machine-readable format. (Article 30(5).)

### [X].16 Online register of data structures and formats

The Provider maintains an up-to-date online register of all data structures and data formats, the relevant standards, and open interoperability specifications in which the Customer's exportable data is available. The register is published at: [INSERT — register URL]. (Article 26(b); Article 30(4).)

### [X].17 Information on switching procedures

The Provider provides the Customer with information on the available procedures for switching and porting to the Service, including the available switching and porting methods and formats, and any restrictions and technical limitations that are known to the Provider. This information is available at: [INSERT — URL]. (Article 26(a).)

## Clause [Y]. International access transparency

### [Y].1 Jurisdiction and measures statement

The Provider's statement of the jurisdiction(s) to which the ICT infrastructure deployed for processing data under this contract is subject, together with a general description of the technical, organisational and contractual measures adopted by the Provider to prevent international governmental access to or transfer of non-personal data held in the Union where such access or transfer would create a conflict with Union law or with the national law of the relevant Member State, is published at: **[INSERT — Article 28 statement URL]**. (Article 28(1).)

### [Y].2 Listing in this contract

The URL referenced in [Y].1 is hereby listed in this contract pursuant to Article 28(2).

### [Y].3 International requests for non-personal data

If the Provider receives a third-country court, tribunal, or administrative authority decision requiring transfer of or access to non-personal data held in the Union, the Provider shall:

- (a) take all adequate technical, organisational and legal measures to prevent transfer or access where such would conflict with Union law or the national law of the relevant Member State (Article 32(1));
- (b) only transfer or grant access where the conditions of Article 32(2) (international agreement) or Article 32(3) (cumulative no-treaty conditions) are satisfied;
- (c) provide the minimum amount of data permissible (Article 32(4)); and
- (d) inform the Customer of the request before complying, except where the request serves law-enforcement purposes and notification would defeat that purpose (Article 32(5)).

**Drafting notes (for the lawyer; remove before signature)**

- Number these clauses to fit the surrounding contract structure.
- Verify that Schedule [Y] is exhaustive at signature and that the Provider's internal mapping supports the listed categories.
- Verify that Schedule [Z] does not contain a provision that effectively defers the switching process.
- The Article 28 jurisdiction-and-measures URL must be live and current before signature; the contract listing under [Y].2 is mandatory.
- For services that are IaaS within the meaning of Article 30(1), [X].15 should be adapted to refer to functional equivalence; for PaaS / SaaS, [X].15 as drafted applies.
- For services qualifying under Article 31, ensure [Z].1 is filled in and the disapplied obligations are listed completely.
- Chapter VI (Art. 25 switching clauses, Art. 26 transparency, Art. 28 international-access disclosure, Art. 29 fees, Art. 30 interfaces / functional equivalence) applies from 12 September 2025 to all DPS contracts, including pre-existing ones. There is no Article 50 grandfather for Chapter VI. The 12 September 2027 grandfather in Article 50 is for Chapter IV (unfair B2B data-sharing terms — Art. 13) only and does not extend any Chapter VI deadline. If this DPS contract also contains B2B data-sharing terms within Chapter IV's scope and pre-dates 12 September 2025, the Chapter IV grandfather may defer Chapter IV's application to those specific terms until 12 September 2027 if the contract is of indefinite duration or has a remaining term ≥ 10 years from 11 January 2024 — verify this overlap independently.
- Member-state implementing law may impose additional requirements; verify independently.
- Sectoral overlays (DORA for financial-services customers, NIS2 for essential entities) may impose additional ICT-third-party requirements; verify independently.
