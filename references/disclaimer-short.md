# Short disclaimer (appended to every output)

The renderer (and the model, on chat answers) appends this text at the end of every deliverable. It is intentionally short so it fits in a single block at the end of a memo or template without dominating it. Two paragraphs: the staleness stamp first (so it is visible at a glance), the legal disclaimer second.

---

**Sources verified:** Regulation (EU) 2023/2854 + European Commission Data Act FAQ v[X.Y], as of [verified_as_of date]. The Commission may have published amendments or FAQ revisions since this date. Before relying on this draft, confirm currency at [EUR-Lex Regulation 2023/2854](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/eli/reg/2023/2854) and the [European Commission Data Act FAQ](https://digital-strategy.ec.europa.eu/en/faqs/data-act-explained-frequently-asked-questions). If your copy of this skill is more than a few months old, check Lawvable for a newer release.

**Disclaimer.** This document is a starting-point draft generated by an information and drafting-assistance tool. It is not legal advice and is not a substitute for advice from a qualified lawyer admitted in the relevant jurisdiction. The European Commission Data Act FAQ is non-authoritative. This document does not address sectoral lex specialis (including, without limitation, Reg. 2018/858, MDR, DORA, NIS2, AI Act) or member-state implementing law; the lawyer must independently verify those overlays.

---

## Variable substitution

The renderer substitutes:

- `[X.Y]` — the FAQ version from `assets/source/_versions.json`
- `[verified_as_of date]` — the `last_check_date` from `_versions.json`

If those values are unavailable, fall back to:

- `[X.Y]` → "unknown, verify before use"
- `[verified_as_of date]` → "unknown, verify before use"

## When to use

Always. Every output, chat or Word, gets this two-paragraph block at the end. The staleness stamp is the lawyer's primary signal that this is a versioned static snapshot, not a live-updating tool. Do not omit it. Do not paraphrase it. Do not summarise it. The verbatim text and the live URLs are what put the lawyer on notice.

## When not to use

Never not. Even short lookups get it. The heavier full-text disclaimer in `LICENSE` is for distribution metadata; the short version above is for in-deliverable use.
