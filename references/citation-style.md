# Citation style

How the skill cites sources. Consistency matters because lawyers will copy citations into their own briefs.

## Regulation

**First citation in any document** — full title:

> Regulation (EU) 2023/2854 of the European Parliament and of the Council of 13 December 2023 on harmonised rules on fair access to and use of data and amending Regulation (EU) 2017/2394 and Directive (EU) 2020/1828 (the "Data Act"), Article 3(2).

**Subsequent citations** — short form:

> Data Act, Art. 3(2).

Or, where unambiguous:

> Reg. Art. 3(2). Reg. Art. 25(2)(d). Reg. Art. 4(7). Reg. Recital 31.

**Always include the sub-point** when one exists. "Art. 4" is too coarse; "Art. 4(7)" is precise. For paragraphs with letters, write "Art. 25(2)(d)" — never "Art. 25(2)d" or "Art. 25.2.d."

**Recitals** are cited as "Reg. Recital 31" or "Data Act, Recital 31." Do not abbreviate as "Rec."

## FAQ

**Always frame the FAQ as non-authoritative.** Examples of correct framing:

> The Commission's interpretation in EC FAQ Q22a, which is not authoritative, supports …

> Per the Commission FAQ v1.4 (22 January 2026), Q58a (non-authoritative) …

**Citation form** — "EC FAQ Q[number]" or "FAQ Q[number]" once context is established.

**Question-letter sub-references** are common (Q22a, Q25a, Q25b, Q58a). Cite them precisely.

**Always include the FAQ version** in any deliverable that is going to be circulated:

> Citations to FAQ Qxx refer to EC FAQ on the Data Act, version 1.4 (22 January 2026).

## Model contractual terms / SCCs

> Commission Recommendation on standard contractual clauses for data sharing and cloud computing contracts under the Data Act, OJ [reference], Annex (model contractual terms / SCCs), 19 November 2025.

Short form: "Commission SCCs."

## "Data Act Explained" page

A non-authoritative Commission overview page. Cite only as background context, never as legal authority.

> European Commission, "Data Act Explained" (digital-strategy.ec.europa.eu, snapshot of [date]).

## Other EU instruments referenced by the skill

- **GDPR** — Regulation (EU) 2016/679. First use full; thereafter "GDPR."
- **DMA (gatekeepers)** — Regulation (EU) 2022/1925. First use "Digital Markets Act, Regulation (EU) 2022/1925"; thereafter "DMA."
- **Trade Secrets Directive** — Directive (EU) 2016/943.
- **Type-approval Reg. (motor vehicles)** — Regulation (EU) 2018/858.
- **MDR (medical devices)** — Regulation (EU) 2017/745.
- **DORA** — Regulation (EU) 2022/2554.
- **NIS2** — Directive (EU) 2022/2555.
- **AI Act** — Regulation (EU) 2024/1689.

## Verbatim quotations

Always direct quotes for definitional or operative text:

> Article 2(8) defines a "data processing service" as: "a digital service that is provided to a customer and that enables ubiquitous and on-demand network access to a shared pool of configurable, scalable and elastic computing resources of a centralised, distributed or highly distributed nature that can be rapidly provisioned and released with minimal management effort or service provider interaction."

Pull the quote from `assets/source/regulation-2023-2854.md` — never reproduce from training data.

Use ellipses sparingly and only with `[...]`. Mark inserted clarifications with square brackets: `[the Data Holder]`.

## Cross-citations within a deliverable

"See paragraph 2.3 above" or "see paragraph 4.1 below." Number paragraphs predictably so cross-references hold even if the document is reordered.

## Footnote format (when used)

```
1   Reg. Art. 25(2)(d) (notice period of two months).
2   EC FAQ Q56 (non-authoritative; Commission view that the customer
    has the right to extend the transition period).
3   Reg. Recital 31 (defining "serious economic damage" as "serious
    and irreparable economic loss").
```

## Citations the skill must NEVER use

- "The Data Act says you must…" — too loose.
- "Article 4 requires the data holder to…" — sub-point missing.
- "The FAQ confirms…" — without the "non-authoritative" qualifier.
- "Recital 31 mandates…" — recitals interpret; they do not mandate.
- "Per the Commission, the answer is X." — the FAQ is not authoritative.
